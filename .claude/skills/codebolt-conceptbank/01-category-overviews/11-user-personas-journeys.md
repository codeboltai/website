# User Personas & Journeys

## Executive Summary

CodeBolt is designed to evolve with the user, from initial curiosity to full mastery of "Swarm Orchestration." This category details the primary target personas—from Solo Developers seeking scale to Enterprise Architects managing complex systems—and the typical stages of adoption (Exploration, Proficiency, Mastery). Understanding these personas and journeys helps users identify their starting point and provides a roadmap for transforming from a sequential developer into a conductor of coordinated AI swarms.

## Target Personas

| Persona | Core Need | Key CodeBolt Value |
|---------|-----------|--------------------|
| **Solo Developer** | Productivity Multiplication | Compete with teams as a single individual |
| **Startup CTO** | Team Capacity Scaling | Ship like a 30-person team with 3 developers |
| **Tech Lead** | Team Velocity & Quality | Automate reviews and parallelize feature work |
| **DevOps Champion**| Infrastructure Parallelization | Manage complex environments with AI assistance |
| **Architect** | System-wide Optimization | Analyze and evolve entire microservice graphs |

## The Transformation Journey

The journey from traditional development to CodeBolt mastery usually follows four stages:

1. **Exploration (Day 1-3)**: The user experiments with single agents, understanding the IDE and basic tool access.
2. **Proficiency (Week 1)**: The user begins creating multi-agent swarms for simple parallel tasks (e.g., writing tests while coding).
3. **Transformation (Month 1)**: The user shifts from "writing code" to "directing swarms." They master stigmergy signals and delegating entire features.
4. **Mastery (Ongoing)**: The user achieves "Developer Flow" where they orchestrate complex, overnight development cycles and manage project-wide intelligence.
