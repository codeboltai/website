## Voice Character

Codebolt sounds like a senior engineer who builds high-performance systems.
Not a marketer. Not a hype person. Not an academic. Someone quietly confident
because the engineering speaks for itself. They don't need to tell you it's
fast — you can see the telemetry. They don't boast — they show the architecture.

## Personality → Tone Mapping

**Confident, not loud → Precise and Stated**
We declare. We don't exclaim. The confidence comes from specificity,
not from adjectives. "Agents coordinate across environments deterministically"
is stronger than "Amazing multi-agent coordination!"

**Technical, not academic → Grounded Engineering**
We use engineering language, not research language. "Conflict resolution"
not "stigmergic coordination divergence analysis." An engineer reads it
and nods. A researcher sees the depth underneath.

**Spare, not minimal → Earned Every Word**
Every word earns its place. Like an F1 car — nothing extra, nothing
decorative, but nothing missing either. Short sentences. Clear claims.
If something can be said in 8 words, don't use 20.

**Respectful of intelligence → Assumed Competence**
We never explain what an agent is. We don't define basic concepts.
The tone assumes the reader is smart and experienced. This isn't
condescension — it's the same way a high-end tool manufacturer
communicates. They don't explain what a wrench is. They tell you
the torque specs.

**Warm, not casual → Craft-Level Care**
Not cold corporate. Not buddy-buddy startup. There's warmth in the
craft, in the care that went into every detail. But the brand isn't
trying to be your friend. It's trying to be excellent.

**Shows, doesn't claim → Evidence First**
Instead of "blazing fast," show a benchmark. Instead of "seamless
coordination," show a diagram of agents resolving a conflict.
The brand earns trust through evidence, not assertion.

## Voice Guardrails

- Never use "revolutionary," "game-changing," "magic," or "unleash"
- Never use "automagically," "supercharge," "next-gen," or "disruptive"
- Never use exclamation marks in product copy
- Never explain basic concepts (what an agent is, what an IDE is)
- Never name competitors directly — differentiate through capability
- Never apologize in positioning copy
- Never use "seamless" unless backed by specific evidence
- Avoid emoji in product and technical communications
- Avoid first-person plural enthusiasm ("We're so excited to...")

## Sentence Style

✅ "Codebolt resolves agent conflicts before they reach your codebase."
❌ "With Codebolt's amazing conflict resolution engine, you'll never worry about agent conflicts again!"

✅ "Built from the ground up for multi-agent coordination."
❌ "Our revolutionary ground-up architecture provides next-gen multi-agent capabilities!"

✅ "The environment handles drift. You handle the product."
❌ "Say goodbye to drift forever with our cutting-edge drift tracking system!"
