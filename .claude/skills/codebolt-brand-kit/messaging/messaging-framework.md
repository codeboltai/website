## Messaging Hierarchy

### Layer 1 — Hero (3-second classification)
**Title:** The High-Performance Environment for AI Agents.
**Subtitle:** Precision-engineered so agents align better, conflict less, and scale further.

*Purpose:* Instant mental bucket placement. The reader knows: this is an IDE,
it's for AI agents, it's about performance and scale. They can immediately
answer "is this for me?"

### Layer 2 — Thesis (10-second differentiation)
**Core insight:** Scaling agents is an engineering problem, not a prompting problem.
Agents don't need better models — they need a better environment.

*Purpose:* Contrarian positioning. Separates Codebolt from every tool chasing
bigger models or better prompts. Reframes the problem.

### Layer 3 — Description (30-second understanding)
Codebolt is a ground-up IDE precision-engineered for AI agents at scale.
It handles what breaks every other multi-agent system: coordination drift,
cross-environment conflicts, agent misalignment, and state fragmentation.
Designed for engineers who treat AI as infrastructure — not just assistance.

*Purpose:* Concrete capability story. The reader now understands what the
product does and why it's architecturally different.

### Layer 4 — Proof (deep-dive credibility)
Architecture diagrams. Benchmarks. Technical deep-dives on drift tracking,
stigmergy-based coordination, narrative versioning, multi-environment
reconciliation. Research papers and methodology.

*Purpose:* Engineering credibility. For the reader who wants to understand
*how* before committing.

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Homepage
- Lead with Layer 1 + Layer 2
- Show, don't tell: architecture diagram or live visualization early
- Layer 3 as the explanatory section
- Layer 4 as deep-dive sections (expandable or linked)
- End with clear CTA, no hype language
- Tone: confident, spare, precise

### Technical Documentation
- Layer 4 depth throughout
- Assume engineering competence
- State what, when to use, tradeoffs
- No marketing language in docs
- Tone: direct, technical, helpful

### Blog / Articles
- Lead with the contrarian insight (Layer 2)
- Support with architecture details (Layer 4)
- Can be more conversational than product copy
- Share genuine engineering challenges and solutions
- Tone: thoughtful, technical, honest

### Social Media
- One idea per post
- Lead with insight, not announcement
- Share benchmarks, architecture details, engineering decisions
- No emoji-heavy announcements
- No "excited to share" openings
- Tone: spare, confident, technical

### Investor / Enterprise
- Lead with Layer 2 (thesis)
- Support with market framing: the gap between AI promise and delivery
- Position as infrastructure layer, not feature set
- Emphasize model-agnostic, enterprise-grade coordination
- Tone: strategic, evidence-based, forward-looking

### Onboarding / In-Product
- Assume the user is capable but new to Codebolt specifically
- Guide without condescending
- Progressive complexity: simple start, depth available
- Tone: warm, precise, respectful
