The Problem with Today's AI Development Tools

Every AI coding tool today is a single-player tool. One AI, one developer, one task at a time.

The Single-Agent Ceiling

Copilot, Cursor, ChatGPT—they all give you one AI helper. That's like having one talented employee who can only work on one thing at a time. Helpful? Yes. Transformative? No.

The Amnesia Problem

AI assistants forget everything between sessions. Every conversation starts from zero. They can't learn your coding standards, remember decisions, or build on previous work.

The Context Window Trap

LLMs see only a tiny fraction of your codebase. They suggest changes in isolation, breaking dependencies they can't see. System-level thinking is impossible.

The 8-Hour Constraint

Development stops when developers log off. Your codebase sits untouched for 128 hours each week. AI could work continuously, but current tools don't let it.
