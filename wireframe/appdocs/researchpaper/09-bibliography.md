# Bibliography and References

## Overview

This bibliography provides comprehensive references for the stigmergy-based multi-agent coordination system research. The sources span multiple disciplines including multi-agent systems, swarm intelligence, software engineering, complexity theory, and biological inspiration.

## Core References

### 1. **Stigmergy and Biological Inspiration**

#### Foundational Papers
- **Grassé, P.-P.** (1959). "La reconstruction du nid et les coordinations inter-individuelles chez Bellicositermes natalensis et Cubitermes sp. La théorie de la stigmergie: Essai d'interprétation du comportement des termites constructeurs." *Insectes Sociaux*, 6, 41-80.
  - *Original formulation of stigmergy theory*

- **Deneubourg, J.-L., Goss, S., Franks, N., Sendova-Franks, A., Detrain, C., & Chretien, L.** (1991). "The dynamics of collective sorting robot-like ants and ant-like robots." *From Animals to Animats*, 356-365.
  - *Mathematical models of stigmergic coordination*

- **Theraulaz, G., & Bonabeau, E.** (1999). "A brief history of stigmergy." *Artificial Life*, 5(2), 97-116.
  - *Comprehensive review of stigmergy research*

#### Modern Applications
- **Parunak, H. V., Brueckner, S., & Odell, J.** (2002). "Swarm coordination." *Engineering Self-Organising Systems*, 77-98.
  - *Early application of stigmergy to engineering systems*

- **Camazine, S., Deneubourg, J.-L., Franks, N. R., Sneyd, J., Theraulaz, G., & Bonabeau, E.** (2001). *Self-Organization in Biological Systems*. Princeton University Press.
  - *Comprehensive treatment of self-organization principles*

## Citation Style

This bibliography follows the APA 7th edition format for consistency. All references should be cited in the main text using the author-date format (e.g., "Dorigo & Stützle, 2004").

## Notes on References

1. **Historical Context**: Foundational papers from the 1950s-1990s established the theoretical basis for stigmergy and multi-agent systems.

2. **Recent Advances**: Papers from the 2000s-2020s show practical applications and modern implementations.

3. **Cross-Disciplinary**: References span computer science, biology, engineering, and social sciences to provide comprehensive coverage.

4. **Implementation Focus**: Technical references support the practical implementation aspects of the system.

5. **Standards Compliance**: References to relevant standards ensure the system meets industry best practices.

This bibliography provides a solid foundation for further research into stigmergy-based multi-agent coordination systems and serves as a comprehensive resource for understanding the theoretical and practical aspects of the field.
