Coordination Protocol

Stigmergy-Based Coordination BREAKTHROUGH

Self-organizing intelligence inspired by nature. Agents coordinate through environmental signals—no central orchestrator required. First implementation of biological coordination principles in AI software development.

Stigmergy is indirect coordination through environmental signals—the same mechanism that allows ant colonies to build complex structures without a central commander. In Codebolt, agents leave "pheromone" signals that other agents can sense and respond to, creating emergent organization. Published Research: "StigmergySwarm: Multi-Modal Environmental Coordination for Large-Scale AI Agent Swarms" (AAMAS 2026).

Pheromone System Architecture

Agents deposit nine distinct pheromone types to signal state, priority, and intent. Other agents sense these signals and adapt behavior accordingly. Each pheromone type has specific semantics and decay rates.

importance
Signal job priority and urgency
Decay: Slow (0.1)
request_split
Signal job should be decomposed
Decay: None (Permanent)
saturation
Signal job is heavily worked on
Decay: Fast (0.2)
takeup_interest
Signal interest in taking up work
Decay: Fast (0.2)
task_not_ready
Signal blocking dependencies
Decay: Medium (0.15)
available
Signal blockers resolved
Decay: None (Permanent)
files_blocked
Signal file reservations
Decay: None (Permanent)
workingonit
Signal active work in progress
Decay: Medium (0.1)
reviewadded
Signal review completion
Decay: None (Permanent)

Smart Job Coordination Patterns

Intelligent task management where agents claim work, signal intentions, and avoid conflicts automatically through emergent coordination patterns.

🔒 Intent-Based Locking

Prevent conflicts before they occur through declaration-based coordination. Agents announce intent to modify files, preventing race conditions and data corruption.

Research Impact: 66.7% reduction in coordination conflicts compared to reactive locking systems.
🔄 Autonomous Task Decomposition

Agents collectively decide when tasks need splitting through "request_split" pheromone accumulation. When intensity exceeds threshold, tasks automatically decompose into sub-tasks.

📊 Adaptive Coordination Selection

Context-aware mechanism selection based on swarm load, task complexity, and agent diversity. System dynamically chooses optimal coordination approach.

Research Impact: 23% improvement over single-mechanism approaches.
