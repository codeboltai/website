Core Concept

Stigmergy:
The Environment IS
the Coordination

Agents coordinate through environmental signals, not direct communication. Like ant colonies, markets, and institutions—complex behavior emerges from simple local rules.

The Concept

What is Stigmergy?

Stigmergy (from Greek: stigma "mark" + ergon "work") is indirect coordination through environmental modification. An agent modifies the environment, another agent perceives that modification and responds accordingly.

In ant colonies, ants deposit pheromone trails that guide other ants to food. No ant knows the global plan. No ant communicates directly. Yet the colony achieves sophisticated foraging behavior.

[Ant Colony Analogy]

🐜 Ant finds food → Deposits pheromone
🐜 Other ants follow trail → Reinforce signal
📍 Optimal path emerges
The Problem with Alternatives

Why Not Direct Communication?

O(n²) Overhead

For n agents to communicate directly, you need n² message pairs. At 100 agents, that's 10,000 potential conversations. Unmanageable.

Meaningless Messages

Most pairwise communications have no value. Agent A working on auth doesn't need to know Agent B's linting status.

Overwhelming

With direct communication, agents spend more time communicating than working. The coordination itself becomes the bottleneck.

Implementation

9 Pheromone Types

Digital pheromones with different purposes and decay rates, organized into 5 temporal layers.

Permanent (No Decay)

available — Blockers resolved, ready for work
request_split — Task needs decomposition
files_blocked — File reservation
reviewadded — Review complete

Temporal (With Decay)

importance — Priority signaling (slow decay)
workingonit — Active work indicator (slow decay)
task_not_ready — Blocked by dependencies (medium)
saturation — Congestion warning (fast decay)
takeup_interest — Intent to claim task (fast decay)
Lifecycle

Pheromone Lifecycle

1

Deposit

Agent deposits pheromone with type, intensity (1-10), and decay rate.

2

Decay

Intensity decays over time: I(t) = I₀ × e^(-λt). Different rates for different needs.

3

Aggregate

Multiple deposits sum together. Consensus builds through accumulated signals.

4

Action

When threshold exceeded, behavior triggers. Priority emerges from collective signaling.

Parallels

Stigmergy in Nature and Society

🐜 Ant Colonies

Pheromone trails for foraging, nest building, and defense. Millions of ants, zero central coordinator.

📈 Markets

Price signals coordinate millions of economic actors. Supply and demand emerge from local transactions.

🏛️ Institutions

Laws, company policies, religious rituals—rules that persist while individuals come and go.

🧬 Biology

Hormones, immune response, metabolism. Trillions of cells coordinating through chemical signals.
