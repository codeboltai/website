Why We Built This

The Problem That
Inspired Codebolt

Software development is fundamentally stuck. Despite advances in AI, companies still struggle with the same problem: they can't build software fast enough.

The bottleneck isn't the technology—it's human bandwidth. Developers can only work on one thing at a time. They go home at night. They context-switch between tasks.

Current AI tools like Copilot help—maybe 20-30%. But they're still single-player tools. One AI, one developer, one task at a time. That's not transformation. That's incremental improvement.

The Insight

What if instead of one AI assistant, you could deploy swarms of specialized agents—each with its own role, its own memory, its own expertise?

What if these agents could coordinate with each other, like ant colonies, without needing constant human direction?

What if development became a 24/7 operation?

That's Codebolt.

Research

Research-Backed Architecture

Codebolt is built on solid research foundations, combining insights from swarm intelligence, distributed systems, and AI coordination.

Stigmergy

Indirect coordination through environmental signals, inspired by ant colonies. Proven in biological systems for millions of years.

Externalized Memory

Solving context limitations at the harness level, not inside agents. Based on cognitive architecture research.

Multi-Agent Systems

Building on decades of research in distributed AI, consensus mechanisms, and agent coordination.
