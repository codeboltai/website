Control

6 Levels of Control

From gentle guidance to emergency halt. Intervene at the level appropriate to the situation.

1 Passive Surfacing

Wait for the swarm to surface issues through escalation. Most hands-off.

2 Request Priority

Use pheromones to shift agent attention. Gentle nudge without direct orders.

3 Direct Task

Assign specific jobs to specific agents. Stronger intervention.

4 Request Termination

Ask an agent to stop its current work. Goes through approval workflow.

5 Direct Halt

Immediately stop an agent. Bypasses normal workflows.

6 Policy Change

Modify rules that govern all agent behavior. Systemic intervention.

Challenge

The Accountability Gap

AI agents move fast. They can make dozens of decisions before you've finished your coffee. How do you maintain human oversight without becoming a bottleneck?

The answer: Layered oversight interfaces. Heatmaps show aggregate activity. Job Maps show work distribution. Agent Panels show individual details.

You zoom in when drift accumulates. You surface issues before they compound. Full audit trails ensure you can always understand what happened.

📊 Job Map — Work distribution
🤖 Agent Panel — Individual detail
