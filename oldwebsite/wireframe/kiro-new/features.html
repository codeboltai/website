Coordination

Advanced coordination mechanisms that enable dozens of agents to work together without chaos.

[Stigmergy Icon Placeholder]

Stigmergy-Based Coordination

Inspired by how ant colonies self-organize, Codebolt uses stigmergy—indirect coordination through environmental signals—to enable agents to work together seamlessly.

How It Works

Agents leave "pheromone trails" in the form of signals, markers, and state changes. Other agents detect these signals and adjust their behavior accordingly. No central orchestrator needed.

This enables emergent coordination: agents naturally avoid conflicts, discover opportunities for collaboration, and self-organize around priorities. The system scales elegantly—50 agents coordinate as smoothly as 5.

[Diagram Placeholder: Stigmergy Coordination Flow]

50 agents working together without chaos. Emergent intelligence from simple rules. No bottleneck orchestrator.

[Deliberation Icon Placeholder]

Agent Deliberation

For important decisions, agents don't just act—they deliberate. Multiple agents can vote on architectural decisions, code approaches, and priority changes.

How It Works

When an agent encounters a decision with significant implications, it can initiate a deliberation process. Other relevant agents contribute perspectives, weigh trade-offs, and vote on the best approach.

Deliberation records are preserved, creating an audit trail of why decisions were made. You can review, override, or adjust the deliberation parameters to match your team's decision-making style.

Agent Economy

Agents operate within an economy with karma and reputation systems. High-performing agents earn trust and get assigned more important tasks.
